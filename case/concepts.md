# Case Concepts

> v0.1 — July 2026. Four candidates; recommendation at the end. Each is a marketing case designed so the AI/agent requirement (see [spec.md](spec.md)) has real work to do.

---

## Concept 1 — Launch campaign for a real Charlottesville business

**The case.** A local business (restaurant group, retail, services — sourced via Batten's community network) needs a launch or growth campaign: new location, new offering, or a repositioning. Teams get a real founder, real constraints, a real budget number.

**Teams deliver.** Segmentation and targeting analysis, campaign strategy and creative direction, a working agent that executes part of the campaign (e.g., generates and schedules channel-specific content variants, or qualifies inbound leads), and a 15-minute recommendation to the founder.

**Why it works.** Real stakes make judging honest — the founder in the room is the tiebreaker no rubric can fake. Mirrors the Tech Innovators Challenge DNA (real partners, solutions that got piloted). Community story is strong for press and for the school.

**Feasibility.** High. Client sourcing is the only real lift; Batten and the Charlottesville chamber network make this tractable. Case can be written in 2–3 pages plus a founder interview.

---

## Concept 2 — Go-to-market for a Batten portfolio venture

**The case.** An early-stage venture from Batten's ecosystem needs its first serious GTM plan: ICP definition, positioning, channel strategy, launch sequencing. Teams work from the venture's actual materials and a founder Q&A session.

**Teams deliver.** Positioning and ICP work, GTM plan with budget allocation, an agent performing a growth function for the venture (outbound content engine, onboarding assistant, competitive-intel monitor), founder-facing presentation.

**Why it works.** Dovetails exactly with Batten's role as idea-source. The venture gets genuinely valuable work product; the best team's artifact could keep running after the event — a story every sponsor and every school comms office wants.

**Feasibility.** High, contingent on one willing venture. Slightly harder judging (early-stage ambiguity) — rubric handles this.

---

## Concept 3 — Market Copilot to small businesses (sponsor meta-case)

**The case.** The prospective sponsor's own GTM problem: AI assistant adoption among SMBs lags enterprise. Teams design a campaign to move SMB owners from awareness to habitual use — using the product itself as the toolchain.

**Teams deliver.** SMB segmentation, adoption-barrier analysis, campaign strategy, and an agent built on the sponsor's platform that demonstrates the value proposition it markets (self-referential by design).

**Why it works.** Maximum sponsor appeal — the sponsor gets 30+ MBA perspectives on a live strategic problem plus platform usage. The self-demonstrating structure ("the campaign artifact *is* the product pitch") is memorable.

**Trade-off.** Weakest neutrality — the event reads as a sponsor marketing exercise if this is the only case. Best used as a sponsor-optional twist or a second track, not the sole case.

---

## Concept 4 — UVA-adjacent nonprofit or NIL campaign

**The case.** A Charlottesville nonprofit's donor/volunteer campaign, or an NIL (name-image-likeness) marketing strategy for a UVA athletics context — both have direct precedent from the Tech Innovators Challenge outcomes.

**Teams deliver.** Audience strategy, campaign plan, an agent handling a real operational piece (donor-segment messaging, volunteer onboarding flows, NIL content calendar), stakeholder presentation.

**Why it works.** Mission energy, easy university approval path, low IP friction. NIL angle is topical and media-friendly.

**Feasibility.** High for nonprofit; NIL requires athletics-department coordination (slower).

---

## Recommendation

**Primary: Concept 1 or 2** — real client, real stakes, no sponsor-conflict, Batten-native sourcing. **Concept 3 works as a sponsor-optional twist** (e.g., a bonus prize for the best use of the sponsor's agent platform) rather than the main case. Concept 4 is the fallback if client sourcing stalls.

A useful forcing question for choosing: *which case gives the winning team an artifact that's still running a month after the event?* That after-life is what separates this competition from every other case comp on the calendar.
