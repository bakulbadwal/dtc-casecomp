# Judging Rubric

> v0.1 — July 2026. Four dimensions, weighted so the business case still dominates — this is a case competition with a build requirement, not a hackathon with slides.

## Weights

| Dimension | Weight | One-line test |
|---|---|---|
| Business recommendation | 40% | Would the client act on this? |
| Working artifact | 25% | Does the agent do a real job, live? |
| AI leverage & process | 20% | Did the team use AI *skillfully*, and can they show their work? |
| Communication | 15% | Was the room persuaded? |

## Scoring guide (1 / 3 / 5 anchors)

### Business recommendation — 40%
- **1** — Generic strategy that could apply to any client; numbers hand-waved; ignores stated constraints.
- **3** — Sound segmentation and channel logic; budget math checks out; recommendation is actionable but safe.
- **5** — Insight the client didn't have walking in; trade-offs made explicitly and defended; the client asks for the deck afterward.

### Working artifact — 25%
- **1** — No working artifact, or a demo of a template with the client's name pasted in.
- **3** — Agent performs its function on realistic inputs; visible seams; team understands its failure modes.
- **5** — Agent does a job the client would actually deploy; grounded in real client materials; handles an unscripted judge prompt. *(A live-demo failure with a sound design and honest diagnosis scores 3, not 1 — reward engineering judgment, not demo luck.)*

### AI leverage & process — 20%
- **1** — Process log missing or vague ("we used Copilot for research"); no evidence of human judgment over AI output.
- **3** — Clear division of labor between humans and AI; log shows at least one AI error caught and corrected; sensible agent design choices.
- **5** — The team's *system* of working is itself impressive — thoughtful decomposition, verification habits, honest accounting of what AI couldn't do. This is the dimension that teaches every team in the room something.

### Communication — 15%
- **1** — Deck read aloud; demo disconnected from the recommendation.
- **3** — Clean narrative; demo integrated at the moment it proves the argument.
- **5** — The demo *is* the argument; Q&A handled with command; a judge quotes the presentation later.

## Mechanics

- Judges score independently before deliberating; averaged, weighted, tie broken by Working Artifact score (that's the event's thesis).
- Suggested panel of 4: one sponsor representative, one faculty/Batten, one practitioner/operator, one case-client representative when applicable.
- **Disclosure norm:** AI use is required and documented — the process log is submitted evidence, not a confession. This should be stated in the case packet in exactly those terms.
