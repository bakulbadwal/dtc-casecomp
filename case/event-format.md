# Event Format

> v0.1 — July 2026. Skeleton with decision points flagged. Dates deliberately unset — see open items.

## Arc

| Stage | When | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| **Kickoff** | Day 0 | Case drop + client intro; sponsor keynote (15 min); hands-on agent-building workshop (90 min) — the workshop is what makes the Tier 2 requirement fair for non-technical teams |
| **Build window** | Days 1–12 | Teams work; two sponsor office-hours sessions; a mid-window "artifact check" (optional, non-scored) so no team discovers a licensing/access problem at hour 11 |
| **Submission gate** | Day 12 | Deck (PDF) + 3-min demo video + AI-process log; completeness-checked on intake |
| **Finals** | Day ~14 | Shortlist of 4–5 teams; 15-min presentations with live demo + 10-min Q&A; judge deliberation; awards |

## Sponsor touchpoint map

What a sponsor gets, concretely — worth writing into the sponsorship conversation:

1. Naming on the event ("presented by …")
2. Kickoff keynote slot
3. Two judge seats (finals)
4. Office hours during build window = structured access to every participating student
5. Resume book of participants (opt-in)
6. Platform-usage story: "N MBA teams shipped working agents on our platform in two weeks"
7. Optional: bonus prize for best use of sponsor platform (clean way to add sponsor presence without bending the main rubric)

## Prizes

Suggested: 1st $3,000 / 2nd $1,500 / 3rd $500, plus platform credits if the sponsor offers them. (Typical school-level range; calibrate to what the sponsorship actually closes at.)

## Open items (decisions owned by DTC leadership)

- [ ] **Date** — fall vs. spring; check against Innovate @ Darden, recruiting season, and FY/SY exam calendars
- [ ] **Scope** — Darden-only vs. UVA-wide (changes judging load, sponsor value, and room size)
- [ ] **School approval path** — career services / student affairs sign-off for a sponsored event with employer contact
- [ ] **Case client sourcing** — via Batten (see [concepts.md](concepts.md)); needs one committed client ~6 weeks out
- [ ] **IP terms** — if a real client is used: who owns team output? (Simple one-paragraph release in the case packet solves this — decide early)
- [ ] **Licensing** — sponsor-provisioned platform environments per team (the hard dependency; see [spec.md](spec.md))
- [ ] **Prize funding** — sponsor cash vs. club budget vs. split
- [ ] **Judge panel** — confirm 4 seats (sponsor ×1–2, Batten/faculty ×1, operator ×1, client ×1)

## Lead-time reality check

Working backward from case drop: sponsor agreement signed **8+ weeks out** (their legal/budget cycles), licenses provisioned and tested **3 weeks out**, case written and client committed **4 weeks out**, registration open **3 weeks out**. If the sponsor conversation is happening now, the earliest comfortable case drop is roughly 10 weeks away.
