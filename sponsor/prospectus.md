## The event, in four sentences

A Darden Tech Club case competition where MBA teams take a real marketing problem — from a real client — and deliver both a boardroom-grade recommendation **and a working AI agent** that executes part of it, demoed live. Not AI-assisted slides: judged deliverables include the running artifact. To our knowledge, no MBA program has run a case competition in this format — the sponsor's platform wouldn't be decoration, it would be what every team builds on. [N] teams, [~30–50] MBA participants, [semester/date window].

## Why the timing matters

Working backward from case drop: sponsor agreement **8+ weeks out**, case written and client committed 4 weeks out, platform access tested 3 weeks out, registration open 3 weeks out. For a [fall/spring] competition, that puts the sponsorship decision in **[month]**. Earlier commitment = more input into case design.

## Why this beats a logo on a banner

Most sponsorships buy visibility. This one buys **evidence**: a cohort of MBAs at a top-10 program shipping working agents on your platform, a judged artifact trail, a resume book of exactly the profile that builds with AI rather than talks about it — and first-mover association with a format other schools will copy.
